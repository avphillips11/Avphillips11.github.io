Section I

The Operating Model

I don't use AI to write emails faster. I use it to fundamentally rethink how a two-person GRC team operates at enterprise scale. Every AI-augmented workflow I run follows one of three patterns, each designed to keep human judgment at the center while compressing the time between intent and output.

I
Draft, Review, Refine

For documentation outputs, I provide AI with source materials (policy text, evidence artifacts, control requirements) and a clear output spec. I review all AI-generated drafts against source materials before use. Iteration is conversational. I refine inline rather than starting over.

II
Synthesize, Structure, Communicate

For analysis tasks (risk scoring, maturity assessments, audit gap analysis), I feed AI structured inputs and request structured outputs: tables, scored assessments, executive summaries. The structure is AI-generated. The inputs and final judgment are mine.

III
Research, Synthesize, Apply

For regulatory and standards work (EU AI Act, ISO 42001, NIST updates), I use AI to synthesize requirements into actionable summaries and map them to my organization's existing control environment. I always cross-reference against primary source documents before acting on AI-generated regulatory summaries.

Section III

Quality Guardrails

AI augmentation only works if the outputs are trustworthy. I apply consistent guardrails across every use case. This is not optional process overhead. It is the reason the 0-findings audit outcome is possible.

I
Source verification on every output

All AI-generated audit narratives and policy drafts are reviewed against primary source materials (standards text, evidence artifacts, contract language) before finalization. Regulatory citations are verified against the actual standard text. I do not rely on AI paraphrasing of requirements for audit deliverables.

II
Human review at every decision point

AI-generated risk assessments are reviewed by me and, where material, by senior leadership before entering the formal risk register. Customer-facing documents go through standard pre-release review regardless of how they were drafted. Nothing ships without human approval.

III
Structured audit trail

I maintain session logs, carry-forward tracking, and standing assumptions codified across sessions. Every AI-assisted output is traceable to its source inputs. The system is designed for transparency and reproducibility, not speed alone.

IV
AI-powered QA before submission

An AI-powered QA process reviews compliance outputs before auditor submission, catching formatting inconsistencies, missing evidence references, and control mapping gaps that manual review can miss. The QA agent is a safety net, not a replacement for human judgment.
